```text
No terminal status, no promotion.
```

A verified object is not operational authority until it terminalizes into a declared authority-bearing status.

# **PART I ŌĆö GENERAL THEORY OF FINITE OBSTRUCTION CALCULUS**

## **Finite Obstruction Calculus Summary**

**Finite Obstruction Calculus (FOC)** is the finite mathematical engine that evaluates a certified regime by representing defects in a finite cochain complex, quotienting out exact repairs, measuring residual obstruction, and classifying the result as exact, survivor, open, lift-required, refused, or otherwise terminalized.

FOC is the local verifier engine.

It computes obstruction and repair. It does not, by itself, grant global authority unless it is embedded inside a finite descent authority packet.



# **PART II ŌĆö GENERAL THEORY OF FINITE DESCENT AUTHORITY**

## **Finite Descent Authority Summary**

**Finite Descent Authority (FDA)** is the doctrine that a proposal earns operational authority only by entering a finite certified regime, being verified inside that regime, and terminalizing into a declared authority-bearing status.

FDA is the global authority doctrine.

FOC supplies a local verification engine. FDA determines when any high-level proposal earns operational authority.

# **FDA CORE CLAIMS**

## **Proposal Non-Authority**

```text
p Ōłł P does not imply Auth(p).
```

A proposal object has no operational authority merely by existing.

It may be a proof sketch, model output, policy, repair, program transformation, database query, transition, or generated answer.

It is still only a proposal until it descends, verifies, and terminalizes.

## **Finite Descent Necessity**

```text
not D implies not Auth.
```

No finite descent packet means no finite operational authority.

Absence of a descent packet is not disproof. It is a typed refusal boundary.

## **Authority Factorization**

```text
Auth = tau Ōłś V Ōłś D
```

More explicitly:

```text
Auth(p) = tau_s(V_s(P_s, eta_s(p)))
```

Authority is not produced by the proposal, route, packet, or verifier alone.

Authority appears only at terminalization.

## **Terminal Authority**

The authority-bearing object is not the answer alone.

It is the terminal pair:

```text

```

An answer without its terminal status has no reasoning authority.
